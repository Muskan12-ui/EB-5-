What is the EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program?

A route to U.S. permanent residency, created by Congress in 1990 — for investors whose capital creates American jobs.

A program built into U.S. law since 1990

The EB-5 Immigrant Investor Program was created by the United States Congress under the Immigration Act of 1990 — the fifth employment-based ("EB-5") category — to attract global investment and create American jobs. By making a qualifying investment that creates at least ten U.S. jobs, an investor, together with their spouse and unmarried children under 21, can obtain a Green Card.

It is not a scheme or a shortcut; it is an established part of U.S. immigration law, strengthened by the 2022 EB-5 Reform & Integrity Act with stronger oversight and investor protections.

How EB-5 works

Three steps, clearly.

1

You invest

You invest the qualifying amount into an approved EB-5 project through a tier-1 U.S. regional-center partner.

2

Jobs are created

Your investment helps create the required U.S. jobs — the heart of the program's purpose.

3

Your family gets residency

You and your eligible family receive a conditional Green Card, then permanent residency.

Who qualifies & how much

EB-5 is merit-neutral. There is no maximum age, no degree requirement, and no IELTS/TOEFL or English test. The focus is your qualifying investment and a lawful, well-documented source of funds. A standard immigration medical exam and the usual admissibility rules still apply.

$800,000

Minimum for a project in a targeted / set-aside area (rural, high-unemployment, infrastructure).

$1,050,000

For a standard project outside those areas. Each investment must create at least 10 U.S. jobs. Amounts are scheduled to rise in 2027.
