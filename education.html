EB-5 isn't just a Green Card. It's your child's head start in America.

The single most persuasive reason Indian parents choose EB-5.

For your children

A child who holds a Green Card enters the U.S. education and career system as a permanent resident — not an international student. That means lower in-state tuition, eligibility for more financial aid, the freedom to work, and a future that doesn't hinge on the H-1B lottery.

Green Card child vs F-1 student
AreaGreen Card (EB-5) childF-1 international student
Right to live in the U.S.PermanentOnly during study + visa validity
University feesMay qualify for in-state tuitionUsually higher international fees
Financial aidMay qualify for aid / loans / work-studyVery limited
WorkingFreelyRestricted (campus / OPT)
After graduationAny suitable jobNeeds OPT, then sponsorship
H-1B lotteryNot requiredOften required

"EB-5 gives your child the same opportunities as a U.S. resident — from university admission and lower tuition to career freedom and long-term settlement." Because children must be unmarried and under 21 to be included (with CSPA rules affecting timing), starting early protects your child's eligibility.
